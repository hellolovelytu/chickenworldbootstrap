Sizes

You may heard about bantam and regular sizes chickens before. Bantam chickens are those weigh below 2lbs (under 1kg). They are cute and light. Regular size chickens have a wide range of weight depending on our breeds. We can get up to 10lbs (about 5kg). On average, a regualr chicken is about 5.7lbs (or 2.6kg).

Temperaments

Choosing chickens are like choosing friends. You have to find a good fit for you and your flock. Chicken has a pecking order that can bring some dramas and heartbreak occasionally. Some are friendly and enjoying being handled. Some like being independent and shy. Some can be fearless. Most of them is curious (like a kid sometimes). Some of the friendliest breeds are:

  1. Silkie
  2. Speckle Sussex
  3. Buff Orpington
  4. Cochin
  5. Australorp

If we haven't proved why you should go find yourself some new chicken friends, here are some more reasons

Recent years, especially during the pandemic, you may hear about "rainbow eggs". Oh, not the colorful Easter eggs rainbow but a more natural, no dye involved kind. People found out that different breeds can lay eggs with a distinct bloom. So yes, not only we give you good nutrious food, but also we are giving you joy from beautiful things we make.

We are also good at fertilizing your garden! More and more people are getting into gardening these days so we are letting you in on our big secret. We poop as we wander. Our manure is called black gold! We should remind you though, don't use it raw. Compost it and use on your plants later, you will have a bountiful garden.

We can be your pest manager! We love digging (for anything shiny and seemingly edible), chasing and eating insects like grasshopper, bettles and their larvaes. See, we help you aerate your soil, turn your compost and control pest population. Here is one of our fellow enjoying a caterpillar!

We also help ourselves with your kitchen scraps! We can use some greens, fruits and grains. See, lessening food waste! You can't live without us, especially if you want to become a more sustainable gardener.
